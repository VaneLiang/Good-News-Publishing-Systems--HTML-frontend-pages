3 fresh litters of Critically Endangered Wolf Pups

By jarvis_admin
Post on 26 March 2021
1 Comment
The North Carolina Zoo on Monday announced the birth of not one, but three litters of critically endangered red wolf pups, totalling 12 pups, born over 12 three days from April 28th to April 30th – according to the zoo both, both the pups and mothers are doing well. Secretary Reid Wilson of the N.C. Department of Natural and Cultural Resources had to say in a press release, “Congratulations to the North Carolina Zoo for playing an essential part in the survival of this critically endangered species – these births are important because many of our wolves, once matured, have been moved to breeding packs to continue to bring this species back from near extinction. Our hope is that more and more wolves can soon be placed into the wild.” The zoo now has 36 red wolves, the second largest pack in the United States after Point Defiance Zoo and Aquarium in Tacoma, Washington. According to the zoo, only 15 to 20 red wolves remain in the wild, and they all are in eastern North Carolina. Red Wolves are considered the most endangered canid in the world. In turn the births come with several firsts. First, this is the first time the zoo in Asheboro has welcomed three litters of pups in a single spring. Second, for the first time in two decades, one of the litters was born on the red wolf public habitat, giving zoo guests a rare chance to view the pups for a limited time, according to the zoo. The pups most likely will be visible starting in mid-June, when they begin to venture outside of the den. The wolf family will be moved to the non-public breeding area when the pups are older and weaned from their mother, the zoo said. The other two litters were born in non-public viewing areas of the zoo.
